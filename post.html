the intersection.

a manifesto, in one breath.

I am not a designer. I am not a coder. I am not an entrepreneur. I am the intersection.

From building forts in the backyard to building worlds online. From Minecraft servers to media campaigns. The throughline was always the same: make things, and make them feel like something.

Forts taught spatial design before I had the word for it. Minecraft taught environment, system, monetization. YouTube taught media literacy by accident. Graffiti, fashion, tattoos taught that identity is a public statement, not a private feeling. Servers and ranks taught interface and economy. Then came the websites, the campaigns, Blender, Three.js — and at some point the tools stopped being separate disciplines and started being a single language.

most designers can make things look good. far fewer understand internet culture, gaming psychology, community mechanics — the language of the online world.

That is the unfair advantage. Not the software. Not the trend cycles. The fact that the medium and I were raised together.

Design earns cash now. Brand builds future equity. Tech scales both. The formula is simple. The execution is a life's work.

This site is not a portfolio. It is not a CV. It is the archive — a personal universe that grows one room at a time, and a record of someone who decided, at 23, to stop apologizing for being good at three things at once.

Welcome inside. Stay as long as you want. The door doesn't lock.
