The Unfair Advantage

a study, on internet-native as discipline.

Internet-native isn't a generation. It's a design discipline. Being born after 2000 doesn't grant you the advantage having actually run the systems does.

the shape of the argument
Most designers use the internet. Far fewer understand its grammar. The grammar is specific and almost never taught: that identity is plural (alts aren't deception, they're frequency separation; MVX / tjbz / eyew is a feature) that aesthetics coexist rather than compose into one brand (a moody account and a shitpost account are the same person) that communities form around mechanics, not values (rank trees, scarcity drops, parasocial proximity) that content has layers surface, lore, meta, in-joke that posts have half-lives, drops have moments, eras have anthems that status is nonlinear and reputation moves between platforms.

Designers who learned this from books got it later, partial, often wrong. Designers who built minecraft servers learned it native rank economies, plugin UI, retention mechanics, the whole thing. Same for thumbnail kids on YouTube, fashion-flip Instagram, fandom Tumblr in 2014. The "schooling" was running the systems.

The punchline
But native isn't the same as good. Plenty of internet-native people are just chronically online. The advantage only counts if you do the design work to translate what you noticed. Otherwise it's just memory.

the advantage isn't being from this generation.
it's having paid attention.
